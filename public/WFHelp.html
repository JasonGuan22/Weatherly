How to Use Weatherly

Welcome to Weatherly! This guide will help you make the most out of our application.

Getting Started

To begin using Weatherly, follow these simple steps:

  1. Navigate to the Home page to check the current weather in your area.
  2. Use the search form to enter your city name.
  3. Click on Get Weather to view the latest weather updates.
  4. Explore the Air Pollution page to see detailed pollution levels and health recommendations.

Checking Weather Forecast

Here's how you can check the weather forecast:

  • On the Home page, enter your city name in the search bar.
  • Click the Get Weather button.
  • View the current temperature, weather conditions, and other relevant data displayed on the dashboard.
  • Navigate through different sections to see weekly forecasts and additional weather details.

Viewing Air Pollution Data

To access air pollution information:

  1. Go to the Air Pollution page via the navigation menu.
  2. Enter your location in the provided form to fetch pollution data specific to your area.
  3. Review the Air Quality Index (AQI), PM2.5, and PM10 levels displayed.
  4. Check out the health recommendations to stay safe during high pollution periods.
  5. Use the interactive map to visualize pollution levels in different regions.

Understanding Air Quality Index (AQI)

The AQI is a standardized indicator used to communicate how polluted the air currently is or how polluted it is forecast to become. Here's a quick overview:

  • Good (0-50): Air quality is satisfactory, and air pollution poses little or no risk.
  • Moderate (51-100): Air quality is acceptable; however, there may be a risk for some people, particularly those who are unusually sensitive to air pollution.
  • Unhealthy for Sensitive Groups (101-150): Members of sensitive groups may experience health effects. The general public is less likely to be affected.
  • Unhealthy (151-200): Everyone may begin to experience health effects; members of sensitive groups may experience more serious health effects.
  • Very Unhealthy (201-300): Health warnings of emergency conditions. The entire population is more likely to be affected.
  • Hazardous (301-500): Health alert: everyone may experience more serious health effects.

Troubleshooting

If you encounter any issues while using Weatherly, try the following solutions:

  • Unable to fetch data: Ensure that your internet connection is stable and try refreshing the page.
  • Incorrect location data: Double-check the spelling of your city name or ensure you're using the correct coordinates.
  • API issues: If the data isn't loading, it might be an issue with our data provider. Please try again later.
  • Other problems: Contact our support team at support@weatherly.com for further assistance.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • How accurate is the weather forecast?
    Weatherly uses reliable data sources to provide accurate forecasts, but weather can be unpredictable.
  • Can I track pollution in multiple cities?
    Yes, you can search for and track pollution levels in any city by entering its name or coordinates.
  • Is my data secure?
    Yes, Weatherly ensures that all user data is handled securely and in accordance with our privacy policy.
